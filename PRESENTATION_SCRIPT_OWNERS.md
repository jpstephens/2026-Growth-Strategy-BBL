**Current State Metrics**

**Say this:**

**Customer Concentration**

**Say this:**

# ACT 2: OWNERSHIP (Slides 6-9)
**Frame: Leadership owns the structural problems**

---

## SLIDE 6: How We Got Here
**Leadership Takes Responsibility**

**Say this:**
"This slide is critical for the employee meeting. We're taking explicit ownership of the structural failure. We asked Patrick to hunt new business while also managing operations. That's an impossible job. When I present this, I need us all aligned that we're not blaming Patrick - we're owning that leadership set up the wrong structure."

---

## SLIDE 7: Two Jobs, One Person
**The Problem**

**Say this:**
"The practical reality: Patrick starts prospecting, gets interrupted by ops issues, and ends up making 10 calls instead of 40. Growth stalled because there was no protected time for sales activity. This is fixable."

---

## SLIDE 8: Specialization Drives Productivity
**Why Sales & Ops Are Incompatible**

**Say this:**
"The underlying principle: sales and operations require different mindsets. Sales is future-focused, operations is present-focused. When one person does both, operations always wins because it's urgent. Sales gets deprioritized even though it's what drives growth."

---

## SLIDE 9: The Growth Trajectory
**The Math of Divided Attention**

**Say this:**
"The math is straightforward. 40 calls/day yields ~4-5 new customers/month. 10 calls/day yields maybe 1. We've been operating at 10-call capacity. The plan creates 40-call capacity."

---

# ACT 3: VISION (Slides 10-14)
**Frame: The target and how roles enable it**

---

## SLIDE 10: The Goal
**December 2026 Target**

**Say this:**
"The target: $100K monthly net profit by December. That requires 65-70 active customers at 4+ loads each, with $200+ margin per load. It's aggressive but achievable if we execute."

---

## SLIDE 11: Patrick's Role & Targets
**100% Sales Focus**

**Say this:**
"Patrick's new role: 100% sales. Protected calling 8am-noon daily. Target is 200 dials/week yielding 4-5 new customers/month. He owns acquisition through first successful load, then hands off to David.

The key commitment from us: we do NOT pull him into operations during protected time. Chris, that means you're his coach and escalation point. If something's on fire at 10am, it goes to Vin, not Patrick."

## SLIDE 12: David's Role & Targets
**100% Operations Focus**

**Say this:**
"David owns operations completely after handoff. All load execution, carrier relationships, customer communication. Targets are 95%+ on-time delivery, sub-2-hour issue resolution.

Vin, you're his direct support. Daily syncs, and you initiate the ops coordinator hire before he hits 120 loads/month. We don't wait until he's drowning."

---

**Customer Ramp Model**

**Say this:**
"Important context on customer value: they don't arrive at full volume. Month 1 is 1-2 loads (trial). By month 3, it's 8-12 loads. By month 6, 15-25 loads. This is why early acquisition matters - January customers contribute meaningful volume by summer. October customers barely contribute before year-end."

## SLIDE 14: Top Customer Profiles
**Customer Profitability**

**Say this:**
